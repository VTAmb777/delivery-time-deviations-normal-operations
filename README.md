# Delivery Time Deviations Under Normal Operational Conditions

## Overview
This repository contains a final research report examining delivery time deviations in logistics systems under statistically defined normal operational conditions.

The study challenges the assumption that delivery delays are primarily caused by extreme events such as heavy traffic or adverse weather and demonstrates that delays persist even during routine operations.

- Descriptive statistics  
- Pearson correlation analysis

- One-way ANOVA  
- Percentile-based definition of normal operational conditions  
- Composite operational normality score analysis

## Key Insight
Delivery delays remain largely unaffected by increasing levels of operational normality, indicating the presence of latent, system-level inefficiencies in routine logistics operations.
